COVID-19 Air Pollution Dashboard


COVID-19 has had effects on health (mental, physical, and emotional), societies and socialisation, and economies across the globe. One of the greatest observed implications of the pandemic and government/public response has been on the environment. There have been numerous headlines reporting on the decreased pollution levels, clearing of smog in significant cities, and the entrance of wild animals into urban structures.

Here, you will be able to access and understand the changes in primary air pollutants for 10 cities. These cities are international cities across the world which have had significant activity during the COVID-19 pandemic - either in their outbreaks or their response strategies.

Fun Facts and Figures

  • Mumbai's median co level dropped from 7.9 in January 2020 to 2.65 in May 2020. A sharp drop by almost half occurred in March 2020, when the lockdown was imposed.
  • The median co level in Los Angeles dropped from 6.4 in January 2020 to 2.4 in May 2020. A similar drop also occurred in March 2020. California had gone into lockdown effective 19 March 2020.
  • Sao Paulo, currently experienced 4,782 of the total 16,856 deaths in Brazil, has not experienced any change in co levels. A lockdown was imposed at the beginning of May 2020.
  • The median co level in London dropped from 4.6 in January 2020 to 2.8 in May 2020. A significant decrease in the median co level occurred between March and April 2020 - from 4.9 to 3.2. A stay-at-home order, with the closure of non-essential businesses, was announced in the last week of March.
  • Seoul experienced gradual reductions in the median co level so far in January - a drop of 1.1 occured every month till April 2020, after which so far the co level has dropped by 1. South Korea has had one of the best strategies and epidemic control program. It is one of the few nations that had experienced a spike in cases, yet had not imposed partial or total lockdowns.
  • Christchurch experienced the exam same change in median co levels as London between March 2020 and April 2020 as well - median co level dropped from 4.9 to 3.2. A nationwide lockdown in New Zealand was also implemented in the last week of March.
  • Tehran, with significantly high co levels compared to the other cities, experienced a drop in co levels between February 2020 and March 2020 from 27 to 24. Iran had not gone into lockdown till the end of March 2020. A huge spike in new cases (from ~400 to ~9000) was experienced in the first week of March. By 19 March, 18,407 total cases were confirmed.
